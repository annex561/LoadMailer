# Lease-to-Own Owner-Operator Program — Term Sheet

**Status:** Draft for transportation-attorney review. Non-binding.
**Purpose:** Defines the business terms for placing a driver in a company-owned truck on a lease-to-own basis, operating under the carrier's authority. The attorney converts this into the binding Lease-Purchase Agreement + Independent Contractor Operating Agreement, compliant with 49 CFR Part 376 and applicable state lease-purchase law.

---

## 1. Parties

- **Carrier / Lessor:** [Company legal name], holder of MC/DOT authority. Recommended: a separate LLC for the leasing arm, isolated from the operating-carrier liability.
- **Driver / Lessee:** Independent owner-operator, 1099 contractor (not an employee).

---

## 2. The Package ("Business in a Box")

The driver is leasing the carrier's titled truck and, for the term of the lease, operates it on the carrier's rails:

- Carrier's MC/DOT **authority**
- Carrier's **insurance** policy (truck added as a covered unit)
- Carrier's **factoring** company
- Carrier's **dispatch** (freight sourcing)
- Carrier's **truck** (leased toward ownership)

These are coupled **by asset ownership**, not by a forced-dispatch mandate: while the truck is titled to the carrier and on the carrier's insurance, it can only legally run under the carrier's authority. The driver retains the right to decline a load and to take approved home time (preserving independent-contractor status). On payoff, the truck and the driver are fully released.

---

## 3. Financial Terms

### 3.1 Up-front (paid before keys)

| Item | Amount | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Truck down payment | **$5,000** | Driver's equity stake. Non-refundable. Forfeited on default. |
| First-month insurance | **$1,500** (set to actual quoted rate) | Prepays first month's coverage so the carrier never fronts the premium. |
| **Total to start** | **$6,500** | |

No credit check. The carrier is secured by holding title and controlling the cash flow, not by the driver's credit score.

### 3.2 Truck price, payment, term

- **Term:** 36 months (3 years).
- **Weekly truck payment:** **$185/week** ($800/month, billed weekly so no large month-end hit).
- **Total of payments:** $5,000 down + (156 weeks × $185) ≈ **$33,800** over the full term.
- **Truck-pricing rule:** Each truck must be priced so that $5,000 down + $800/month pays it off exactly at 36 months. Source trucks in the ~$25,000–$30,000 range so the envelope closes and the markup is preserved. Do **not** put a driver in a truck whose price the 3-year / $800 schedule can't retire.
- **Early buyout:** Permitted at any time for the remaining payoff balance.

### 3.3 What the 20% covers

The carrier retains **20% of gross revenue** on dispatched loads, covering **dispatch + use of the carrier's authority**. The 20% does **not** cover fuel, factoring, or insurance.

### 3.4 What the driver pays

- **Fuel** — driver's cost, paid from his net.
- **Factoring** — driver uses the carrier's factor; factoring fee is the driver's cost (deducted in the settlement).
- **Insurance** — see Section 5.

---

## 4. Weekly Settlement & Deduction Waterfall

The carrier factors all loads; funds land in the carrier's account first. The carrier pays the driver **once per week** with an **itemized settlement statement** (required under Part 376) showing every deduction. Deductions come off in this order:

1. **20%** dispatch/authority fee
2. **Insurance** (carrier's policy + flat weekly rate)
3. **Truck payment** ($185/week)
4. **Maintenance escrow** ($75/week)
5. **Factoring fee** (driver's portion)
6. **Remainder paid to driver** (driver pays fuel from this)

### 4.1 Slow-week / shortfall rule

If a week's revenue does not cover all deductions:

1. The **20% and insurance always come out first** (carrier's authority and policy — non-negotiable).
2. **Truck payment and escrow** come out of whatever remains.
3. Any unpaid truck-payment/escrow portion **rolls into arrears**, automatically caught up on the next good week.
4. Arrears reaching the ceiling in Section 7 = default.

---

## 5. Insurance

- The truck is added as a covered unit on the **carrier's commercial auto + cargo policy** (required, since the truck runs under the carrier's authority). The driver cannot carry a separate policy for this operation.
- **Carrier provides:** primary auto liability + cargo.
- **Driver pays (via weekly deduction):** the cost of physical-damage (collision/comp) coverage on the truck, plus a flat margin. Quoted as a fixed weekly line.
- **Deductibles:** any claim caused by the driver's negligence — the **deductible is the driver's responsibility**.
- **Safety-out clause:** the driver must maintain a clean MVR/CSA record. A driver whose record or at-fault claims raise the carrier's premiums, or threaten the carrier's insurability, may be **removed from the program**. (The carrier's insurance record is the asset the entire program depends on.)

---

## 6. Factoring

- The driver uses the **carrier's factoring company**. Bringing outside factoring is not permitted while leasing, because carrier-controlled factoring is what makes the deduction waterfall automatic.
- Volume rebate, if any, from the factor accrues to the carrier.

---

## 7. Default — Performance-Based (not "missed payment")

Because the carrier auto-deducts from each settlement, a traditional "missed payment" cannot occur. Default is instead defined by **non-performance**:

1. **Idle truck:** truck sits **7 consecutive days** without running a load, absent an approved reason (verified breakdown or approved home time).
2. **Arrears ceiling:** accumulated unpaid truck-payment + escrow reaches **3 weeks' worth** (≈ $780).
3. **Abandonment:** driver disappears with or abandons the truck — immediate default.
4. **Abuse / neglect:** skipped required maintenance, or damage beyond normal wear.

### 7.1 Consequence of default

Down payment forfeited, accrued equity forfeited, truck returns to the carrier, and the carrier may re-seat it with another driver.

---

## 8. Maintenance & Escrow

- **Maintenance escrow:** $75/week, accumulated from the driver's settlement. The escrow is the driver's funds, used for repairs and preventive maintenance on the truck.
- **Required PM schedule:** oil changes, DOT inspections, tire service on a set calendar, with receipts submitted. Compliance is tied to the agreement.
- **Inspection rights:** the carrier may inspect the truck on a defined schedule to confirm condition and maintenance.
- **Escrow at completion:** remaining escrow balance is reconciled to the driver at payoff (or applied to outstanding repairs/arrears on default).

---

## 9. Completion / Title Transfer / Release

On payment of the full 36-month schedule (or early-buyout payoff):

- **Title transfers** to the driver. Truck owned free and clear.
- The driver is **fully released** — free to run under his own authority, use any factoring, work with anyone, or leave entirely.
- No continuing obligation to the carrier.

---

## 10. Driver Eligibility / Vetting

Gate at entry (cheaper than a claim):

- Valid CDL, minimum [2] years verifiable OTR experience
- Clean MVR / acceptable CSA
- DOT drug test passed
- $6,500 up-front confirmed

---

## 11. Required Legal Review

Before use, a transportation attorney must:

- Convert this term sheet into the binding **Lease-Purchase Agreement** + **Independent Contractor Operating Agreement**.
- Confirm **49 CFR Part 376** compliance (lease contents, settlement transparency, escrow handling, chargeback rules).
- Confirm compliance with the applicable **state lease-purchase / lease-to-own** statutes.
- Pressure-test **worker-classification** language so the driver remains a bona fide independent contractor (no forced-dispatch / no-refusal language).
- Confirm the **escrow handling** meets Part 376 escrow-accounting requirements (statement of escrow, interest, return on termination).
