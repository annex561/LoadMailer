# Direct-Shipper & Dedicated-Lane Target List
**Carrier profile:** small/growing fleet, dry van primary + reefer secondary. Footprint = mid-South hub (Memphis/Nashville/Atlanta) radiating across TN, KY, GA, AL, MS, AR, MO, and lower Midwest (southern IL/IN/OH). Occasional FL and Carolinas. No Northeast, no West.

**How to read difficulty:** Easy = small carrier can get set up in days to weeks with $100k cargo / $1M auto and clean authority. Medium = needs ~6-12 mo authority age, a TMS onboarding, or a broker intro. Hard = strict routing guide / mega-carrier capacity, pursue only with a track record or a 3PL sponsor.

## Tier 1 — Go after now
Mid-market, small-carrier-friendly, dense freight on the green lanes.

### 1. Tractor Supply Company
- HQ Brentwood, TN. DCs incl. Maumelle/Little Rock AR + regional SE/Midwest DCs.
- Ships: farm/ranch/pet retail, store replenishment. Dry van.
- Fit: TN-hub outbound to stores across the whole green zone. Steady year-round.
- Door: inbound/transportation team + brokers running store pools / dedicated store routes.
- Difficulty: Medium (appointment discipline, TMS). Strong once 6+ mo clean authority.

### 2. Dollar General
- HQ Goodlettsville, TN; 30+ DCs, many in footprint.
- Ships: discount consumables, store replenishment. Dry van.
- Fit: densest rural SE/Midwest store network — DC-to-store lanes blanket your zone, constant backhauls into DCs.
- Door: direct private-fleet supplement, faster via dedicated 3PLs running store pools.
- Difficulty: Medium (tight windows; easier via 3PL sponsor first).

### 3. McKee Foods (Little Debbie)
- Collegedale, TN HQ + bakery; second bakery Hixson/Chattanooga.
- Ships: snack cakes, shelf-stable palletized. Dry van.
- Fit: high-volume Chattanooga outbound to DCs/retailers across SE + lower Midwest. Family-owned, works with regional carriers.
- Door: direct Collegedale traffic dept; also SE grocery brokers.
- Difficulty: Easy-Medium.

### 4. Bush Brothers (Bush's Beans)
- Chestnut Hill, TN production + Augusta, GA plant.
- Ships: canned beans — heavy, shelf-stable. Dry van.
- Fit: East-TN outbound to grocery DCs across SE/Midwest. Weight-friendly round trips.
- Door: direct traffic dept Chestnut Hill; grocery 3PLs.
- Difficulty: Easy-Medium.

### 5. Mohawk Industries
- Calhoun/Dalton, GA flooring cluster.
- Ships: carpet, LVT, laminate, rugs. Dry van.
- Fit: Dalton "carpet capital" — huge GA outbound, strong SE/Midwest lanes. Anchor for Atlanta-radius outbound.
- Door: logistics dept; spillover via broker pool. Pitch a specific GA→(Nashville/Memphis/Midwest) lane.
- Difficulty: Medium.

### 6. Shaw Industries
- Dalton, GA (Berkshire Hathaway).
- Ships: flooring/surfaces. Dry van.
- Fit: same Dalton density as Mohawk — pair them to build a GA outbound book leaving Atlanta loaded.
- Door: transportation + contracted brokers; dedicated-lane pitch GA→SE/Midwest.
- Difficulty: Medium.

### 7. Electrolux / Frigidaire
- Springfield, TN cooking campus (consolidated US appliance mfg).
- Ships: ranges, ovens — bulky palletized. Dry van.
- Fit: north-of-Nashville plant, outbound to appliance DCs/retailers across SE/Midwest. Inbound components = good backhaul.
- Door: direct plant logistics; spillover via broker pool.
- Difficulty: Medium.

### 8. Wayne-Sanderson Farms (poultry) — REEFER
- Oakwood, GA HQ; plants Decatur AL, Danville AR, GA/NC.
- Ships: fresh/frozen chicken. Reefer.
- Fit: multi-plant GA/AL/AR base, steady reefer outbound to grocery DCs in SE/Midwest.
- Door: plant shipping offices; mostly temp-controlled brokers / reefer 3PLs first.
- Difficulty: Medium (reefer + food-grade standards).

## 3PL / Dedicated Programs — FASTEST path to committed freight for a small fleet

### A. J.B. Hunt — Dedicated Contract Services + Carrier 360 brokerage
- Heavy SE/Midwest dedicated base. Carrier 360 is one of the easiest digital onboards to start hauling committed lanes immediately. Register → ask reps about SE dedicated lanes.
- Difficulty: Easy (brokerage) → Medium (dedicated assignment).

### B. Schneider — Dedicated + Owner-Operator/Fleet-Owner program
- Strong SE/Midwest dedicated accounts; explicitly recruits fleet owners. Clean fit for a growing small carrier.
- Difficulty: Medium.

### C. Ryder — Dedicated Transportation + Owner-Operator
- Manages dedicated fleets AND automotive/industrial inbound across the SE — backdoor into the Nissan/GM/VW parts freight you can't get direct.
- Difficulty: Medium.

### D. NFI Industries — Dedicated / contract carrier
- Large SE/Midwest dedicated retail + CPG accounts; recruits contract carriers.
- Difficulty: Medium.

> Strategy: onboard 2-3 of these (A-D) THIS month for immediate committed lanes + backhaul coverage, then convert the history into direct Tier-1 contracts.

## Free/cheap tools to find more shippers on these lanes
- ImportYeti (free) — BOL/import data; find consignees through Savannah/Charleston/Memphis intermodal needing domestic outbound.
- Panjiva / ImportGenius (paid, trials) — deeper BOL filtering.
- FreightWaves SONAR — lane-level tender volume / headhaul-backhaul index; find tight (high-rate) SE/Midwest markets.
- State manufacturer directories (free gold mine): TNECD (TN), Georgia Dept. of Economic Development (GA), Made in Alabama (AL), AEDC (AR), plus MS/KY/MO commerce depts. Plants by county + product + headcount.
- ThomasNet (free) — industrial manufacturer directory by product + state.
- Reference USA / Data Axle (free via public library) — manufacturers/distributors by NAICS + zip radius around Memphis/Nashville/Atlanta.
- Chamber of commerce manufacturer rosters (Memphis, Nashville, Chattanooga, Huntsville, Birmingham, Atlanta, Little Rock).
- CBRE / JLL / Colliers industrial reports (free PDFs) — where new DCs are being built (freight before routing guides lock).
- FMCSA SAFER (free) — vet broker authority/age.
- Google Maps satellite of I-40 / I-65 / I-24 / I-75 / I-22 industrial corridors — spot DC clusters, ID tenants.

## The dedicated-lane pitch that works
Lead with consistent capacity on a NAMED lane:

> "We run a dedicated fleet out of the Memphis/Nashville corridor and already move loads weekly on [Atlanta→Nashville]. We can commit guaranteed capacity on that lane at a flat rate, so you stop chasing the spot market every week — and because we backhaul the return leg ourselves, we hold your rate steady. Give me one regular lane for 30 days and I'll show you 98%+ on-time and zero missed pickups."

Why it lands: capacity security is what shippers buy. Your SE density is the unfair advantage (trucks already in their market = structurally lower cost than a national carrier deadheading in). Start with ONE lane, nail the scorecard, expand.

**Equipment flags:** Nucor + Vulcan are flatbed/bulk (out-of-scope). Reefer targets: Wayne-Sanderson, Tyson, Pilgrim's. Everything else dry van.
